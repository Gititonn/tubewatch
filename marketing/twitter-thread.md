**Tweet 3:**
1/ The title format matters more than the topic.

"I ate only eggs for 30 days" (specific, timeframe, personal) → 12x outlier

"Egg diet review" (generic) → 0.8x

Same creator, same topic, 15x difference in performance. The frame is the product.

---

**Tweet 4:**
2/ Outlier videos are almost always SHORTER than that creator's average.

Not by a little — by 20-30%.

Theory: when creators are onto something that genuinely works, they don't pad it. The confidence to be concise correlates with the quality of the idea.

---

**Tweet 5:**
3/ The thumbnail has ONE subject.

Not a collage. Not text + face + product + arrow. ONE thing that makes you stop.

I checked 500 outlier thumbnails. 89% had a single dominant visual element. Complex thumbnails almost never go viral.

---

**Tweet 6:**
4/ The video was published Tuesday–Thursday.

Weekend publishing underperforms by ~30% on average across niches.

Exception: gaming and entertainment. Their audiences are most active on weekends.

Know your niche's peak days.

---

**Tweet 7:**
5/ The outlier video was on a topic the creator had NEVER made before.

Not their usual angle. A new format, a new subject, or a new type of hook.

The algorithm rewards novelty. Creators who copy their own hits rarely replicate them.
